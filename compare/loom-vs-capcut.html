Loom vs CapCut: Which Is Better?

Choosing between Loom and CapCut? Here's a side-by-side comparison to help you decide.

Quick verdict: Choose Loom if you need remote-teams or freelancers. Choose CapCut if you prioritize content-creators or social-media-managers.

Loom

PricingFree / $12.50/mo Business / custom Enterprise
Rating★★★★ 4.4/5

Key Features

  • Instant screen and camera recording with shareable links
  • Viewer analytics with watch time, completion rate, and engagement data
  • Comment and reaction system for async video conversations
  • Video trimming for basic content cleanup
  • Team workspaces for organizing videos by project or department
  • Integrations with Slack, Notion, Jira, and email

Pros

  • Saves hours of meetings — a 3-minute Loom replaces a 30-minute sync call
  • Extremely easy to use: record, stop, share link. That's it. No editing, no rendering, no uploads
  • Viewer analytics show you who watched, how much they watched, and whether they finished
  • Viewer reactions and comments turn one-way recordings into asynchronous conversations
  • Camera + screen combo recordings build trust by showing your face alongside the content

Cons

  • Free tier caps you at 25 videos — fine for trying it out, but you'll hit that wall fast in daily use
  • No editing tools beyond trimming the start and end; you can't cut out mistakes in the middle
  • Expensive per seat for what it does; $12.50/month for a screen recorder feels steep
  • Video quality is capped at 720p on the free tier (Business bumps to 1080p)
  • Occasional upload issues with longer recordings have improved but still happen

CapCut

PricingFree / $7.99/mo Pro
Rating★★★★ 4.3/5

Key Features

  • Free video editor with no watermark on free tier
  • AI auto-captions and speech-to-text
  • Text-to-speech with multiple voice options
  • Background removal and green screen
  • Trending templates and effects library
  • Cross-device project syncing

Pros

  • Free version is incredibly capable — no watermark, no significant feature gates, no time limits
  • AI auto-captions are fast and accurate; essential for silent-viewing social video
  • Text-to-speech with multiple natural-sounding voices is built in and free
  • Works on mobile and desktop with project sync between devices
  • Templates and effects library is massive and updated frequently with trending styles

Cons

  • Privacy concerns due to ByteDance ownership; data handling policies are a concern for some users
  • Desktop version is less polished than the mobile app; the UI feels like a mobile port
  • Advanced editing features (multi-cam, color grading) are basic compared to DaVinci or Premiere
  • Export quality on free tier is capped at 1080p; 4K requires Pro
  • The Pro subscription auto-renews at $7.99/mo and is required for commercial use license

Which Should You Choose?

Both Loom and CapCut are excellent tools in their category. The right choice depends on your specific needs:

  • Choose Loom if: Loom is the async video tool that replaced standup meetings for thousands of remote teams. Instead of scheduling a call, you record your screen, talk through what you're showing, and share the link. Viewers can watch at any speed, leave comments, and respond with their own video. It's simpler than it sounds, and it saves an absurd amount of meeting time.
  • Choose CapCut if: CapCut is ByteDance's video editing app that's become the default for short-form content creators. It's free, it runs on basically any device, and it has the AI features (auto-captions, text-to-speech, background removal) that TikTok and Reels creators need. The trade-off is that it's owned by the same company as TikTok, which raises privacy questions for some.
