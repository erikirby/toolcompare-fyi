ClickUp vs Lark: Which Is Better?

Choosing between ClickUp and Lark? Here's a side-by-side comparison to help you decide.

Quick verdict: Choose ClickUp if you need small-teams or agencies. Choose Lark if you prioritize remote-teams or startups.

ClickUp

PricingFree / $7/mo Unlimited / $12/mo Business
Rating★★★★ 4.2/5

Key Features

  • 15+ project views including list, board, Gantt, calendar, timeline, and workload
  • Docs with collaborative editing and nested pages
  • Whiteboards for visual brainstorming and diagramming
  • Goals with target tracking and progress roll-ups
  • Automations for repetitive task management workflows
  • Built-in email, chat, and clip recording

Pros

  • Feature count is honestly ridiculous — 15+ views, Docs, Whiteboards, Goals, Mind Maps, Clips, Chat, Email
  • Unlimited plan at $7/mo is excellent value given everything included
  • Custom fields and automations are powerful enough to replicate most workflows you'd build in dedicated tools
  • Multiple view types (list, board, Gantt, calendar, timeline, workload) cover almost every use case
  • Dashboards with widgets give a real-time picture of progress across multiple projects

Cons

  • Can feel bloated; the sheer number of features makes it overwhelming for new users
  • Learning curve is steep — expect a week or two of 'where do I find X' before it clicks
  • Performance degrades noticeably with large workspaces (500+ tasks, multiple custom fields)
  • Mobile app is functional but slow and lacks many desktop features
  • Some features feel half-baked compared to dedicated tools (the chat is no Slack, the docs are no Notion)

Lark

PricingFree / $12/mo Pro / custom Enterprise
Rating★★★★ 4/5

Key Features

  • All-in-one suite with messenger, docs, calendar, video, and sheets
  • Video conferencing with 100+ participants on free tier
  • Smart docs with real-time collaboration and sidebar chat
  • Calendar with scheduling automation and cross-platform sync
  • Messenger with threaded conversations, bots, and app integrations
  • Cloud storage with 100GB on free tier

Pros

  • The free tier is almost unbelievable — 100+ participant video calls, 100GB storage, unlimited messaging
  • All-in-one means you don't need separate apps for chat, docs, calendar, and video
  • Messenger integrates deeply with docs; you can co-edit a document while chatting about it in the sidebar
  • Video conferencing quality is reliable and includes features like background blur, recording, and live captions
  • Sheets are surprisingly capable for a free alternative to Google Sheets

Cons

  • Privacy concerns because it's owned by ByteDance (TikTok's parent) — a non-starter for some organizations
  • Less adopted in Western markets, which means fewer community templates and integrations
  • The all-in-one approach can feel overwhelming; there's a learning curve for the full suite
  • Some integrations with Western tools (Salesforce, HubSpot) are less mature than competitors
  • Occasional UI inconsistencies between the different apps within the suite

Which Should You Choose?

Both ClickUp and Lark are excellent tools in their category. The right choice depends on your specific needs:

  • Choose ClickUp if: ClickUp is the project management tool that tries to be everything for everyone. It has more views and features than any competitor — docs, whiteboards, goals, mind maps, chat, email, even a built-in clip-on timer. The ambitious scope is both its greatest strength and its biggest weakness. If you can tame it, it's incredibly powerful.
  • Choose Lark if: Lark is ByteDance's answer to Google Workspace — an all-in-one suite with messenger, docs, calendar, video, and sheets in a single app. It's huge in Asia and gaining traction elsewhere, largely because the free tier is absurdly generous (video calls with 100+ participants, 100GB cloud storage). The elephant in the room is that it's made by the company behind TikTok.
