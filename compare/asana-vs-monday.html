Asana vs Monday.com: Which Is Better?

Choosing between Asana and Monday.com? Here's a side-by-side comparison to help you decide.

Quick verdict: Choose Asana if you need small-teams or marketing-teams. Choose Monday.com if you prioritize small-teams or marketing-teams.

Asana

PricingFree / $10.99/mo Premium / $24.99/mo Business
Rating★★★★ 4.3/5

Key Features

  • Timeline/Gantt view with dependency mapping and critical path analysis
  • Automation builder for recurring workflows and status updates
  • Portfolios for cross-project visibility and resource planning
  • Goals and OKR tracking with progress roll-ups
  • Custom fields, templates, and project briefs for standardized processes
  • Integrations with Slack, Microsoft Teams, Google Workspace, and 200+ apps

Pros

  • The Timeline (Gantt) view is genuinely excellent — drag to adjust dependencies, see critical path, catch bottlenecks early
  • Workflow builder automations are powerful enough to eliminate a lot of manual status-updating busywork
  • Non-technical team members pick it up fast; the UI is intuitive and forgiving
  • Portfolios feature gives managers a bird's-eye view of multiple projects without digging through spreadsheets
  • Goal-setting and OKR tracking ties daily tasks to bigger-picture objectives

Cons

  • Gets expensive per seat once you add Premium or Business for the good features
  • Projects can get visually cluttered fast with too many subtasks, custom fields, and dependencies
  • No native development tools — engineering teams will still want something like Linear or Jira
  • The free tier is quite limited once you need Timeline, advanced search, or automations
  • Notifications can be overwhelming if you don't spend time dialing them in

Monday.com

Pricing$9/mo Basic / $12/mo Standard / $19/mo Pro per seat
Rating★★★★ 4.2/5

Key Features

  • Visual project boards with customizable columns and color coding
  • Automation recipes for workflow triggers and actions
  • Dashboards with real-time widgets for project health visibility
  • Multiple project views (board, timeline, calendar, Gantt, workload)
  • Templates for marketing, sales, HR, operations, and software teams
  • Integrations with 50+ business tools via native connectors and Zapier

Pros

  • Visual boards are intuitive and colorful; non-technical team members understand them immediately
  • Automation recipes handle common workflows (status changes, notifications, task assignments) without coding
  • Templates for every use case — marketing campaigns, sales pipelines, product roadmaps, HR onboarding
  • Dashboards with widgets give a real-time overview of project health across multiple boards
  • Integrations with Slack, Gmail, Teams, and 50+ other tools are well-maintained

Cons

  • Expensive — per-seat pricing adds up; a 20-person team on Standard pays $240/month before any extras
  • No proper Gantt chart on the Basic plan (Timeline view is limited); you need Standard or Pro
  • Free tier is limited to 2 seats, making team evaluation difficult
  • Can be overkill for simple workflows; the visual complexity doesn't always add value
  • Reporting and analytics features are quite basic unless you're on Pro or Enterprise

Which Should You Choose?

Both Asana and Monday.com are excellent tools in their category. The right choice depends on your specific needs:

  • Choose Asana if: Asana is the work management tool that non-technical teams rally around. Where Linear is built for engineers, Asana is built for the marketing, operations, and product folks who need to coordinate complex projects without writing a single line of code. The timeline view alone has saved many a campaign launch.
  • Choose Monday.com if: Monday.com is the visual work management platform that's taken the corporate world by storm. Its color-coded boards, customizable columns, and automation recipes make it accessible to non-technical teams in a way that developer-focused tools never could. The trade-off is cost — it gets expensive fast as you add seats and premium features.
