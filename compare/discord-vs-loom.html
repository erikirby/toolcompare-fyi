Discord vs Loom: Which Is Better?

Choosing between Discord and Loom? Here's a side-by-side comparison to help you decide.

Quick verdict: Choose Discord if you need communities or gamers. Choose Loom if you prioritize remote-teams or freelancers.

Discord

PricingFree / $9.99/mo Nitro
Rating★★★★ 4.4/5

Key Features

  • Voice channels with crystal-clear, low-latency audio
  • Server organization with roles, categories, and granular permissions
  • Text channels with markdown, embeds, and thread support
  • Comprehensive bot API for moderation, automation, and engagement
  • Stage channels for audio-based events and presentations
  • Screen sharing and video calls with up to 25 participants

Pros

  • Free tier is incredibly generous — unlimited messages, history storage, and 25MB file uploads
  • Voice chat quality is excellent; built on Opus codec, low latency, and handles 25+ people without issues
  • Server structure with roles and permissions lets you build complex, tiered communities
  • Bot ecosystem is massive and enables everything from moderation to music to mini-games
  • Stage channels (audio-only events) are great for AMAs, town halls, and community calls

Cons

  • Not designed for formal business communication — threading is limited and message formatting is basic
  • No built-in task management, project tracking, or productivity features
  • Can get noisy fast; a busy server with multiple active channels requires active moderation
  • Nitro's benefits (higher file uploads, custom emojis) are mostly cosmetic for business use
  • Screen sharing quality drops noticeably at higher resolutions compared to Teams or Zoom

Loom

PricingFree / $12.50/mo Business / custom Enterprise
Rating★★★★ 4.4/5

Key Features

  • Instant screen and camera recording with shareable links
  • Viewer analytics with watch time, completion rate, and engagement data
  • Comment and reaction system for async video conversations
  • Video trimming for basic content cleanup
  • Team workspaces for organizing videos by project or department
  • Integrations with Slack, Notion, Jira, and email

Pros

  • Saves hours of meetings — a 3-minute Loom replaces a 30-minute sync call
  • Extremely easy to use: record, stop, share link. That's it. No editing, no rendering, no uploads
  • Viewer analytics show you who watched, how much they watched, and whether they finished
  • Viewer reactions and comments turn one-way recordings into asynchronous conversations
  • Camera + screen combo recordings build trust by showing your face alongside the content

Cons

  • Free tier caps you at 25 videos — fine for trying it out, but you'll hit that wall fast in daily use
  • No editing tools beyond trimming the start and end; you can't cut out mistakes in the middle
  • Expensive per seat for what it does; $12.50/month for a screen recorder feels steep
  • Video quality is capped at 720p on the free tier (Business bumps to 1080p)
  • Occasional upload issues with longer recordings have improved but still happen

Which Should You Choose?

Both Discord and Loom are excellent tools in their category. The right choice depends on your specific needs:

  • Choose Discord if: Discord started as a place for gamers to talk during raids and somehow became a legit team communication platform. It's free, it's feature-rich, and it's where a surprising number of open-source projects, developer communities, and even corporate teams now live. The voice chat quality is genuinely better than most business tools.
  • Choose Loom if: Loom is the async video tool that replaced standup meetings for thousands of remote teams. Instead of scheduling a call, you record your screen, talk through what you're showing, and share the link. Viewers can watch at any speed, leave comments, and respond with their own video. It's simpler than it sounds, and it saves an absurd amount of meeting time.
