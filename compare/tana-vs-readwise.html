Tana vs Readwise: Which Is Better?

Choosing between Tana and Readwise? Here's a side-by-side comparison to help you decide.

Quick verdict: Choose Tana if you need power-users or researchers. Choose Readwise if you prioritize readers or researchers.

Tana

PricingFree / $15/mo Pro / $30/mo Team
Rating★★★★ 4.2/5

Key Features

  • Super-tags for creating structured data schemas within notes
  • Graph database backend for interconnected knowledge systems
  • Daily notes as the primary capture mechanism
  • Node-based editor with infinite nesting and references
  • Powerful search combining structured fields and full-text
  • Workspaces for separating different knowledge domains

Pros

  • Super-tags are genuinely innovative — they let you define structured data schemas inside your notes, turning them into a database
  • Daily notes as the default entry point reduces the friction of deciding where to put things
  • Graph view shows connections between notes in a way that's more useful than Obsidian's (better filtering and navigation)
  • The node-based structure means you can nest and reference content with surgical precision
  • Search is excellent; the combination of structured fields and full-text search is powerful

Cons

  • Learning curve is brutal — expect to spend several hours before it clicks, and days before you're productive
  • No mobile app yet (as of 2025), which is a dealbreaker for on-the-go note-taking
  • Expensive at $15-30/mo for what is ultimately a notes app
  • The interface can feel dense and overwhelming with nested nodes and fields everywhere
  • Smaller community means fewer tutorials, templates, and shared workflows than Obsidian or Notion

Readwise

Pricing$7.99/mo / $89.88/yr
Rating★★★★½ 4.5/5

Key Features

  • Highlight aggregation from 50+ sources (Kindle, Twitter, web, newsletters, PDFs)
  • Spaced repetition review for long-term retention of key insights
  • Export integration with Obsidian, Notion, Roam, and other note-taking tools
  • Reader app for read-later, annotation, and PDF/EPUB reading
  • Ghostreader AI for inline summaries, explanations, and analysis
  • Daily email digest for passive review of recent highlights

Pros

  • Aggregates highlights from everywhere — Kindle, Twitter, Apple Books, Medium, Instapaper, Pocket, newsletters, web articles
  • Spaced repetition review (daily email or app) actually helps you retain what you read
  • Exports to Obsidian, Notion, Roam, Evernote, and most note-taking tools with customizable formatting
  • PDF and EPUB annotation in the Reader app is excellent with highlights, notes, and search
  • Ghostreader AI (built-in) can summarize articles, define terms, and explain concepts inline

Cons

  • No free tier at all — you pay from day one, which limits experimentation
  • The browser extension is functional but less polished than dedicated read-later tools like Matter
  • The two-product model (Readwise + Reader) can be confusing; some features are split between both
  • Spaced repetition review can accumulate backlogs quickly if you're a heavy reader
  • Some integrations require workarounds (not all sources natively support highlighting export)

Which Should You Choose?

Both Tana and Readwise are excellent tools in their category. The right choice depends on your specific needs:

  • Choose Tana if: Tana is the note-taking app for people who found Notion too unstructured and Roam too chaotic. It combines a graph database backend with a structured note-taking interface, letting you build interconnected knowledge systems without losing your mind. The super-tag system is genuinely novel — it lets you define data schemas within your notes. It's powerful, but it demands a lot from you.
  • Choose Readwise if: Readwise solves a problem every knowledge worker has: you read great articles, books, and newsletters, but the insights get lost. Readwise aggregates your highlights from Kindle, Twitter, newsletters, PDFs, and web articles into one place, then resurfaces them with spaced repetition. The Reader app (added in 2023) turns it into a read-it-later tool with the same highlighting and annotation features.
