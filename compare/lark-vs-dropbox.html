Lark vs Dropbox: Which Is Better?

Choosing between Lark and Dropbox? Here's a side-by-side comparison to help you decide.

Quick verdict: Choose Lark if you need remote-teams or startups. Choose Dropbox if you prioritize freelancers or small-teams.

Lark

PricingFree / $12/mo Pro / custom Enterprise
Rating★★★★ 4/5

Key Features

  • All-in-one suite with messenger, docs, calendar, video, and sheets
  • Video conferencing with 100+ participants on free tier
  • Smart docs with real-time collaboration and sidebar chat
  • Calendar with scheduling automation and cross-platform sync
  • Messenger with threaded conversations, bots, and app integrations
  • Cloud storage with 100GB on free tier

Pros

  • The free tier is almost unbelievable — 100+ participant video calls, 100GB storage, unlimited messaging
  • All-in-one means you don't need separate apps for chat, docs, calendar, and video
  • Messenger integrates deeply with docs; you can co-edit a document while chatting about it in the sidebar
  • Video conferencing quality is reliable and includes features like background blur, recording, and live captions
  • Sheets are surprisingly capable for a free alternative to Google Sheets

Cons

  • Privacy concerns because it's owned by ByteDance (TikTok's parent) — a non-starter for some organizations
  • Less adopted in Western markets, which means fewer community templates and integrations
  • The all-in-one approach can feel overwhelming; there's a learning curve for the full suite
  • Some integrations with Western tools (Salesforce, HubSpot) are less mature than competitors
  • Occasional UI inconsistencies between the different apps within the suite

Dropbox

PricingFree / $9.99/mo Plus / $16.99/mo Family
Rating★★★★ 4.2/5

Key Features

  • Cross-platform file sync and cloud storage
  • Smart Sync for on-demand file access without local storage
  • File sharing with link controls (passwords, expiration dates)
  • Dropbox Transfer for large file delivery
  • Paper collaborative documents
  • Dropbox Capture for async screen recordings

Pros

  • Sync is still best-in-class — files appear on all your devices quickly and reliably
  • File versioning and recovery works well; you can restore deleted files for 30 days (180 on Plus)
  • Smart Sync lets you see all your files without downloading them; saves local storage space
  • Dropbox Transfer for sending large files (up to 100GB on Plus) with expiration dates and passwords
  • Paper (built-in docs) is surprisingly good for lightweight collaborative documents

Cons

  • Expensive for what it is — $9.99/mo for 2TB when Google Drive gives you the same for less
  • Recent redesigns have made the desktop app slower and more bloated than it used to be
  • The free tier is very limited (2GB); you'll run out of space almost immediately with any real use
  • No built-in office suite; you still need Google Docs or Microsoft 365 for document editing
  • The push into AI and dash tools feels like feature creep; the core sync experience has suffered

Which Should You Choose?

Both Lark and Dropbox are excellent tools in their category. The right choice depends on your specific needs:

  • Choose Lark if: Lark is ByteDance's answer to Google Workspace — an all-in-one suite with messenger, docs, calendar, video, and sheets in a single app. It's huge in Asia and gaining traction elsewhere, largely because the free tier is absurdly generous (video calls with 100+ participants, 100GB cloud storage). The elephant in the room is that it's made by the company behind TikTok.
  • Choose Dropbox if: Dropbox is the file sync and sharing service that defined the category. It's been around long enough that it's easy to dismiss, but the core product — files that sync across devices seamlessly — still works better than most competitors. Recent additions like Dropbox Dash (universal search), Capture (screen recording), and Replay (video review) show it's trying to evolve beyond just file storage.
