Obsidian vs Tana: Which Is Better?

Choosing between Obsidian and Tana? Here's a side-by-side comparison to help you decide.

Quick verdict: Choose Obsidian if you need freelancers or writers. Choose Tana if you prioritize power-users or researchers.

Obsidian

PricingFree / $25/mo Commercial use
Rating★★★★½ 4.6/5

Key Features

  • Local Markdown-first storage — your notes are plain files, not a database
  • Bidirectional links and graph visualization for discovering connections between ideas
  • 1,500+ community plugins for customization (Kanban, spaced repetition, mind maps)
  • Canvas whiteboard for visual thinking and spatial arrangement
  • Obsidian Publish for turning your notes into a public website
  • Obsidian Sync for encrypted, cross-device syncing

Pros

  • Everything lives on your machine as plain Markdown files — no lock-in, no syncing drama, no format anxiety
  • Plugin ecosystem is massive (1,500+ community plugins) and covers everything from Kanban to mind maps to Pomodoro timers
  • The graph view is surprisingly useful for seeing connections between ideas you'd forgotten about
  • Canvas feature lets you visually arrange notes, images, and links in a freeform whiteboard space
  • Obsidian Sync is genuinely fast and encrypted if you do want cloud access

Cons

  • Syncing between devices requires Obsidian Sync ($5/mo) or doing it yourself with iCloud/Git
  • Steep learning curve — you'll spend hours setting up plugins and workflows before it clicks
  • Mobile app is functional but feels rough compared to the desktop experience
  • The community plugins are great but can break on updates, leaving you hunting through forums at 11 PM
  • No built-in real-time collaboration; it's fundamentally a single-player tool

Tana

PricingFree / $15/mo Pro / $30/mo Team
Rating★★★★ 4.2/5

Key Features

  • Super-tags for creating structured data schemas within notes
  • Graph database backend for interconnected knowledge systems
  • Daily notes as the primary capture mechanism
  • Node-based editor with infinite nesting and references
  • Powerful search combining structured fields and full-text
  • Workspaces for separating different knowledge domains

Pros

  • Super-tags are genuinely innovative — they let you define structured data schemas inside your notes, turning them into a database
  • Daily notes as the default entry point reduces the friction of deciding where to put things
  • Graph view shows connections between notes in a way that's more useful than Obsidian's (better filtering and navigation)
  • The node-based structure means you can nest and reference content with surgical precision
  • Search is excellent; the combination of structured fields and full-text search is powerful

Cons

  • Learning curve is brutal — expect to spend several hours before it clicks, and days before you're productive
  • No mobile app yet (as of 2025), which is a dealbreaker for on-the-go note-taking
  • Expensive at $15-30/mo for what is ultimately a notes app
  • The interface can feel dense and overwhelming with nested nodes and fields everywhere
  • Smaller community means fewer tutorials, templates, and shared workflows than Obsidian or Notion

Which Should You Choose?

Both Obsidian and Tana are excellent tools in their category. The right choice depends on your specific needs:

  • Choose Obsidian if: Obsidian is what happens when you make a note-taking app for people who think deeply about how they think. It stores everything as local Markdown files — no proprietary format, no cloud lock-in — and lets you build a personal knowledge graph by linking your notes together. It's not trying to be Evernote. It's trying to be a second brain.
  • Choose Tana if: Tana is the note-taking app for people who found Notion too unstructured and Roam too chaotic. It combines a graph database backend with a structured note-taking interface, letting you build interconnected knowledge systems without losing your mind. The super-tag system is genuinely novel — it lets you define data schemas within your notes. It's powerful, but it demands a lot from you.
