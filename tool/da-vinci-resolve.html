DaVinci Resolve Review

DaVinci Resolve is the professional video editing software that's free for the vast majority of users. It's the same tool Hollywood uses for color grading, and the free version is so capable that many YouTubers and indie creators never need to upgrade. The learning curve is real — this is not iMovie — but the power is unmatched at any price, let alone free.

Pros & Cons

👍 Pros

  • Free version is absurdly capable — 4K export, unlimited tracks, professional color grading, Fairlight audio tools
  • Color grading tools are the industry standard; the power you get for free is genuinely ridiculous
  • Fusion (visual effects) and Fairlight (audio) are built-in; you don't need After Effects or Audition separately
  • Studio version ($295 one-time) is a bargain compared to monthly subscriptions for Premiere Pro
  • Performance on Apple Silicon is excellent; optimized for M-series chips

👎 Cons

  • Learning curve is steep — expect weeks before you're comfortable, months before you're fast
  • Free version has some limitations: no noise reduction, no 10-bit color (for many formats), no HDR
  • Interface is dense and can feel overwhelming compared to Final Cut or Premiere
  • Not great for quick edits; the startup time and project setup are overkill for a 2-minute social clip
  • Community and tutorials are growing but still smaller than Premiere Pro's ecosystem

Key Features

  • Professional video editing with timeline-based workflow
  • Industry-standard color grading with HDR support
  • Fusion visual effects and motion graphics compositor
  • Fairlight audio production with multi-track mixing
  • Multi-user collaboration for team post-production
  • Free tier with 4K export and professional features

Best Use Cases

  • Professional video editing and color grading on a budget
  • YouTube and content creation with Hollywood-grade tools
  • Post-production workflow for indie filmmakers and video professionals
