Canva Review

Canva is the design tool for people who don't call themselves designers. It's used by everyone from solo creators making Instagram posts to enterprise marketing teams producing global campaigns. The template library is absurdly comprehensive — you'll almost never start from scratch — and the AI features are actually useful rather than gimmicky.

Pros & Cons

👍 Pros

  • Unbelievably easy to use — if you can drag and drop, you can design something that looks professional
  • Template library has hundreds of thousands of professionally designed templates for every format
  • Free tier is genuinely generous: thousands of templates, 5GB storage, and basic design tools
  • Magic Studio AI tools (background removal, magic expand, text-to-image) are impressive for a web tool
  • Brand Kit feature lets you save colors, fonts, and logos for consistent on-brand designs

👎 Cons

  • You'll hit the ceiling fast if you have actual graphic design training — it's not a replacement for Illustrator or Photoshop
  • Exports can feel basic; vector formats are limited and print-quality exports need Pro
  • Branding controls are limited on the free tier, making it hard to maintain consistency in teams
  • Performance bogs down with large, multi-page designs
  • The AI features are paywalled behind Pro (though that's an increasingly common pattern)

Key Features

  • Drag-and-drop editor with thousands of templates
  • Magic Studio AI suite (background removal, expand, text-to-image)
  • Brand Kit for consistent brand colors, fonts, and logos
  • Team collaboration with comments, approvals, and version history
  • Presentation studio with presenter notes and live collaboration
  • Print and merchandise design with production ordering built-in

Best Use Cases

  • Social media graphics in every dimension and format you'll ever need
  • Presentations that don't look like they came from a 1990s template
  • Marketing materials like flyers, brochures, and posters for non-designers
