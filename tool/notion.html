Notion Review

If you've ever wished your note-taking app could also be your project manager, database, wiki, and to-do list rolled into one, Notion is probably what you're looking for. It's become the default workspace for a huge chunk of the freelance world — and for good reason.

Pros & Cons

👍 Pros

  • Molds to whatever workflow you throw at it — kanban boards, wikis, databases, calendars, you name it
  • Templates are genuinely good; the community template gallery alone is worth exploring
  • Free tier is shockingly generous for what you get (unlimited pages, blocks, and collaborators)
  • The block-based editor means you can nest and organize content in ways no other tool lets you
  • Collaboration is smooth — real-time editing, comments, and sharing all work without friction

👎 Cons

  • Drags noticeably once you push past ~200 database entries, especially on mobile
  • No offline access on mobile is a real pain when you're on a train or plane
  • Database queries are surprisingly limited compared to something like Airtable
  • The more you customize, the harder it is for teammates to navigate — there's no guardrails
  • Exporting out of Notion to another tool is messy; they don't exactly make it easy to leave

Key Features

  • Block-based editor that lets you build anything from docs to databases
  • Relational databases with rollups, formulas, and linked views
  • 200+ templates across every use case you can imagine
  • API and integrations with Slack, Zapier, Figma, and more
  • Team collaboration with comments, real-time editing, and permissions
  • AI assistant for writing, summarization, and Q&A (paid add-on)

Best Use Cases

  • Building a second brain / personal wiki that actually works
  • Running a content calendar with editorial workflows and status tracking
  • Creating a shared knowledge base for a remote team
  • Tracking freelance clients, projects, invoices in one dashboard
